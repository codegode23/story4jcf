JCF


Jireh Charity Foundation is an NGO consisting of well intended individuals who's main aim is to provide aids to less privileged individuals mainly orphaned children and widows by providing them with basic needs such as food, clothing, educational materials, vocational skills amongst others. This is to help alleviate their burdens, make their lives better and help equip them with necessary life skills. We are a registered NGO with the Registrar General We aid in bettering the lives of the less privileged and orphaned. We do this by providing basic needs, materials to help enrich their and smiles on their faces through love and unity. Our Organization primarily focuses on children between the ages of zero to eighteen years.

JCF is committed to bringing people together to help mobilize funds and resources to be donated to various children charity homes. These include educational items such as books, pens, pencils, school bags, shoes and school clothes. We also provide hygiene products such as toiletries, sanitary pads amongst others. We regularly organize health screening for for children at the Homes.

Vision

Jireh Charity Foundation aids and equips various children's homes with resources needed to operate and better the lives of young ones in their care. We help with the education and upkeep of kids in rural areas also.

Mission

Our vision is to create a sustainable long term community of individuals who will be committed to the mission of monthly donation and provision of support to various children's homes all year long. Our long term goal is the creation of an all inclusive children's home, run by us, to help all needy children we come across have access to quality life and education for the betterment of themselves and the society they will find themselves.

Our story

JCF started as a student to student organization with the intent of giving back to society's less privileged. A group of friend's had a heart to heart conversation about the woes of children in the HOMES and too it upon themselves to find ways to collate essential items, sort them and donate to Children's Homes. In an effort to mobilize more funds and items, we used friend-to-friend intersections to create awareness and social media platforms to show transparency and updates/progress of our organaizational affairs. We mean well.

Charity Events

Suhum, Koforidua

Our first ever event dubbed "DROP YOUR BIT". On March 6th 2019, we embarked on our first ever donation event at Jehovah Rapha Children's home, Suhum, Koforidua. This marked the launch of our organization as well as our mandate of providing aid to the less privilaged. We basically went frm hostel room to hostel room, to talk to our fellow students to donate an item each from their personal itinerary. Hence the name "DROP YOUR BIT"


Huhunya

After a successful intoductory event in March 2019, we enthusiasically embarked on another charity outreach program in May of the same year at Hour Of Grace Children's Home, Huhunya. we received sponsorship from various individuals including 200pieces of chickens from WonderWings Ghana. The kids loved the meals. We donated some educational items, school bags, books, shoes etc. We also donated some food items and hygiene products.


Suhum, Koforidua

Akuapem Tutu

2020 was a bit tricky. We had Charity programs lined up all year then Covid hit. We had to wait for an “all clear” from our government before we could have any large gatherings of any sort which really hindered our programs. But nonetheless, on the 27th December of 2020, we held another Christmas edition “OUT OF LOVE, WE GIVE”. We visited Trinity Home at Akuapem Tutu in the Eastern Region of Ghana.
JCF together with some of our sponsors like JB Visuals, Klergi Studios, COD properties amongst others embarked on this journey to put smiles on kids in that orphanage home.
We contracted Stand Out Care Medical Team to come along with us to do free medical screening for the children. We also donated drugs to the on-site orphanage nurse to administer to the children after they’ve been confirmed ill and properly diagnosed by a medical officer.
We donated items such as bags of rice, gari, soft drinks, books, pens, pencils, sanitary pads, toilet rolls, soaps and so many essential items. Again, we played games, ate, made memories that will never fade with the wonderful children of Trinity home. We also met other wonderful individuals and HopeKare Foundation who also came to the orphanage home with the same mission as us. Together, we made the day special for the young ones.
We are blessed to carry this mandate we’ve been given. JCF truly CARES!

Nuaso

2022, a blessed year. We started the year by hosting LoveConnect. A subsidiary of JCF. We went along with them together with their items and ours and donated/volunteered in their names. It was a way to raise more awareness about the plights of children in these various Homes. It was a very successful initiative. All year round, we had many people signing up to donate and volunteer at various Orphanage and Children’s Homes.

AKWAMU ABOASA

A glorious morning in the rural countryside the Eastern Region. Specifically Akwamu Aboasa. We touched down in the early minutes of 9am. We were warmly welcome by the chief of the town and his elders. They asked us asked our mission and we humbly obliged. We did our setup and commenced our health screening just before 10am. We commenced with a health talk concerning “THE ARISING MATTERS OF MENTAL HEALTH ISSUES” We first commenced our screening with the aged and disabled and then children. We screened them for various diseases including malaria, tuberculosis and HIV. Routine checks such and BP and glucose level checks were also done. Consultancy by our Medical Officers then followed. Our on-site pharmacist issued medications to person who required them. JCF together with the inhabitants of the town partook in a deworming exercise. We had a setup for our student packages. This was where children of school going ages received various stationery including pens, pencils, rulers and exercise books etc. Older citizens and teens received the student packages plus personal hygiene souvenirs such as sanitary pads, toothbrushes, toothpastes and grooming products. NHIS renewal was done for all persons with expired coverage. This was to encourage them seek medical assistance when needed at a subsidized cost. It was a worthwhile initiative. We had barbers on site to trim and groom interested citizens of Akwamu Aboasa traditional area so they can look their best! Of course, there was refreshment for citizen who partook in the our Drive. We made sure they were properly hydrated and well fed. Our volunteers did an amazing job. All in all, we are grateful to God for his mercies and grace. Our volunteers did a terrific job. We couldn’t have done it without them. A special thanks to the leaders and community of Akwamu Aboasa. JCF, we care. And because we care, We Serve.
